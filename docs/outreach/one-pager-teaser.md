# ComfySeniors One-Pager Teaser

**Purpose:** A single-page PDF sent with cold outreach emails, or handed out after a warm conversation. The reader either acts on it or requests the full 16-slide deck. Designed to fit on ONE letter-sized page (8.5" × 11") with generous whitespace.

**Use cases:**
- Attach to cold campaign email as a lightweight PDF
- Print + hand out at industry conferences
- Link from social bios and email signatures
- Share link after a discovery call ("here's a quick summary")

## Design direction for the PDF version

Hand this copy to Canva, Gamma, or a designer with these instructions:

**Layout:**
- Letter-size, 8.5" × 11", portrait orientation
- Margins: 0.75" top/bottom, 0.6" left/right
- Fits on ONE page — do not let it spill to two

**Typography:**
- Headline ("Your facility is losing..."): Cormorant Garamond 36pt, primary blue #1E3A8A
- Subheadline: Cormorant Garamond italic 20pt, muted gray #6B7280
- Section headers ("The problem..."): Plus Jakarta Sans 14pt, bold, primary blue
- Body: Plus Jakarta Sans 11pt, body color #1F2937
- CTA URL: Plus Jakarta Sans 22pt, bold, lavender #A78BFA, with lavender underline

**Color hits (sparingly):**
- `$6,500` and `$32,500`: loss red #DC2626, bold
- `$297/month` and `$0 placement fees`: lavender #A78BFA, bold
- Section divider lines: thin, 0.5pt, light gray #E5E7EB

**Visual elements:**
- Top-left: small ComfySeniors couch logo (about 60px tall)
- Top-right: "comfyseniors.com" in small muted text
- Thin horizontal rule between each section (not heavy dividers)
- Bottom: lavender CTA block with white text for the URL
- NO stock photos. The page is typography-driven.
- NO gradients, shadows, or decorative graphics.

**The feel:**
Think New York Times op-ed, not sales flier. Serious, confident, clean.
A facility administrator should read it in 90 seconds and walk away
either intrigued or annoyed — never confused.

## Two production paths

**Path A — Canva (free, 20 min):**
1. Sign in to [canva.com](https://canva.com)
2. New → Custom size → 8.5" × 11" inches
3. Paste the copy block above
4. Apply the fonts + colors from the design direction
5. Export as PDF (Print quality)
6. Save to `docs/outreach/one-pager-teaser.pdf`

**Path B — Claude Design (fastest):**
1. Copy the entire copy block (from "Your facility is losing..." through "...America's most honest senior-care directory")
2. Paste into Claude Design with this prefix:
   > *Design a single-page letter-sized PDF one-pager using the following
   > copy verbatim. Brand: ComfySeniors. Primary #1E3A8A, lavender #A78BFA,
   > loss red #DC2626, off-white #F9FAFB background. Fonts: Cormorant
   > Garamond for headlines (serif), Plus Jakarta Sans for body. No stock
   > photos. Typography-driven. Feel: NYT op-ed, not sales flier.*
3. Iterate until it's tight.
4. Export PDF.

**Path C — Figma / Illustrator** (if you want maximum control and have design chops).

## When to use the one-pager vs. the full deck

| Situation | Send the... |
|---|---|
| Cold email campaign, first touch | **One-pager** (attached as PDF or linked) |
| Reply to "send me more info" | **One-pager** + link to full deck |
| After a discovery call | **Full deck** |
| Investor or partnership meeting | **Full deck** |
| Conference handout | **One-pager** (printed, double-sided with a QR code on back) |
| Email signature | Link to the **one-pager** landing URL |
| Social media bio link | Link to the **one-pager** landing URL |

The one-pager is the hook. The deck is the close.
